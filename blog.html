Week 1: Laying the Foundations

February 3-7, 2025

Full Blog

The first week of my internship was all about getting to know the project, setting expectations, and planning the work ahead. It was a crucial phase where we established a solid foundation for the upcoming weeks.

Meeting with the Mentor

To kick off the internship, we had an in-depth discussion with our mentor about the project. We covered key aspects such as:

- Who is responsible for which tasks
- The mentor’s vision for the project
- The necessary deliverables and deadlines
- The available resources and tools

This meeting gave us a clear understanding of what needs to be done and how we will collaborate as a team.

Meeting with the Supervisor

Following our initial discussion, we had a formal meeting with both our mentor and the project supervisor.
This was essential to align our work with the expectations and requirements set by the supervisor. We discussed:

- The project scope and objectives
- Key deadlines and milestones
- The necessary criteria our internship work must meet

Having this clarity from both the mentor and supervisor ensured that we were on the right track from the start.

Assessing Resources and Planning the Setup

After the meetings, we took a hands-on approach by physically inspecting the available resources. This allowed us to:

- Identify the tools and equipment we can use
- Determine what additional resources are needed
- Discuss with fellow interns how we will set up everything

By the end of the week, we had a clear plan for the physical setup and a structured approach for the weeks ahead.

Conclusion

This first week was all about preparation, communication, and organization. Understanding the project’s goals, available resources, and team roles has given us a strong start. With everything now in place, I am looking forward to diving into the actual work in the coming weeks!

Week 2: From Theory to Practice

February 10-14, 2025

Full Blog

This week at my internship has been both educational and hands-on, providing me with valuable insights into SD-WAN technologies and real-world deployments.

Monday – Self-Study

Since I will be attending a three-day course next week on SD-WAN technologies and deployments, I first had to prepare myself by completing the SD-WAN Essentials course. I spent the entire Monday studying the fundamentals to ensure I had a solid understanding before the training sessions. This self-study session covered key topics such as:

- The benefits of SD-WAN over traditional WAN solutions
- How SD-WAN simplifies network management and improves performance
- Security considerations in SD-WAN deployments
- Different architectures and deployment models

It was a lot of information to absorb in one day, but it gave me a strong foundation for the upcoming training sessions.

Tuesday – Hands-On Lab Work

Tuesday was all about practical experience. Together with my fellow interns, I worked in the lab to set up and configure two Aruba EC-L-H appliances in preparation for their deployment in a production environment.

Our tasks included:

- Installing the necessary software and updates on the appliances
- Configuring basic network settings to ensure connectivity
- Testing the appliances in a controlled lab environment
- Cleaning and organizing the lab to maintain an efficient workspace

This hands-on session helped reinforce the theoretical knowledge I had gained the day before. Working directly with the hardware and troubleshooting configurations gave me a much clearer understanding of how SD-WAN solutions are implemented in real-world scenarios.

Thursday – Deployment in a Production Environment

Thursday was one of the most exciting days of the week because we took our configurations from the lab and deployed the appliances in a live production environment. This was a crucial step, as it required us to ensure that everything was properly installed and functioning without disrupting existing network operations.

During the deployment, we:

- Physically installed the Aruba EC-L-H appliances in their designated locations
- Connected them to the network and verified connectivity
- Finalized and fine-tuned the configurations to ensure seamless operation
- Conducted tests to confirm stability and performance

Additionally, we documented the entire setup process by creating a detailed network diagram and writing comprehensive installation and configuration documentation. This documentation will be useful for future troubleshooting, maintenance, and reference by other team members.

Conclusion

This week has been a great mix of theoretical learning and practical experience. Studying SD-WAN Essentials gave me the foundational knowledge I needed, while working in the lab and deploying real appliances helped me understand the challenges and best practices involved in network implementations.
